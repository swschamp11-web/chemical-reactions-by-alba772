Acids and Bases

Acids and bases are important substances in chemistry. They react with each other in a process called neutralization.

What is an Acid?

An acid is a substance that releases hydrogen ions (H+) when dissolved in water.

Examples of Acids

  • Hydrochloric acid (HCl)
  • Sulfuric acid (H2SO4)
  • Nitric acid (HNO3)
  • Acetic acid (vinegar)

Properties of Acids

  • Taste sour
  • Have a pH lower than 7
  • Can react with metals
  • Turn blue litmus paper red

What is a Base?

A base is a substance that releases hydroxide ions (OH-) when dissolved in water.

Examples of Bases

  • Sodium hydroxide (NaOH)
  • Potassium hydroxide (KOH)
  • Calcium hydroxide
  • Ammonia

Properties of Bases

  • Taste bitter
  • Feel slippery
  • Have a pH higher than 7
  • Turn red litmus paper blue

Neutralization Reaction

When an acid reacts with a base, they neutralize each other and form salt and water.

General Reaction:

Acid + Base → Salt + Water

Example Reaction

Hydrochloric acid reacts with sodium hydroxide:

HCl + NaOH → NaCl + H2O

In this reaction:

  • HCl is the acid
  • NaOH is the base
  • NaCl is the salt
  • H2O is water

pH Scale

The pH scale measures how acidic or basic a substance is. The scale goes from 0 to 14.

  • 0–6 = Acidic
  • 7 = Neutral
  • 8–14 = Basic

Common Examples in Daily Life

  • Lemon juice – acidic
  • Vinegar – acidic
  • Baking soda – basic
  • Soap – basic

Conclusion

Acids and bases are important in chemistry and everyday life. Their reactions help in cleaning, cooking, medicine, and many industrial processes.
